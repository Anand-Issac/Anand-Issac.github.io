Experience

I just completed my second coop term as a Software Developer at BlackBerry QNX in Ottawa, ON. I had an amazing experience and made several new friends and professional connections. I worked on the Audio team and my job mainly focused on creating scripts in Python to validate several audio processes such as: priority ducking, channel mapping, volume ramping, and sample rating. I was able to also lead a design change in the audio sample rate process which saves up to 2x memory usage. I learned the importance of keeping strict coding standards to maintain large code bases and the importance of writing neat, clean, easy to follow code. Along with developing cool things, I got to really mature as an individual by living on my own for the first time in my life. Check out my experiences!

My first coop term was as a Software Engineer at Ontario Public Service. At the OPS, I was responsible for developing a file validation framework in Python which parsed through 300,000 records of live data and analyze them in 8 seconds which saves 5 days and $4500 in every 60 day build. I also developed a cheque printing Java program which cut reliance on third party vendors and saves the organization $30,000 every year. I have learned a lot from my coop experiences but growing as a software developer is a lifelong process.
